PROJECT: DR DRONE

The creation of Dr. Drone came from a class project at university. My team Victoria, Rachel and I designed a solution for the Paramedic industry, after being given the brief to create something viable in 10 years and uses an autonomous vehicle or device. With the extreme nature of emergency services being highly stressful and time critical, it is vital that the response times and treatment of patients are as streamlined as possible. Our problem statement is:

"How can we streamline paramedic services to enhance on-the-spot intervention and improve response times, without interfering with the essentials emotional support from a paramedic-patient relationship?"

Solution

We all value freedom and independence, which is why we all appreciate and want a trusting safety net to catch us when we take a tumble in life. Being able to request and search up immediate help from the palm of your hands will finally give you the support you never thought you could have! Quickly search up the most accurate medical solutions, formatted from some of the brightest professionals in Australia, to your general illness. Or, link straight to a 000 call and receive fast efficient help from an intelligent Dr. Drone thatâ€™s personally sent to your picked up location to aid you, whilst you wait for your all-time trusting paramedics to come. We will constantly be connecting you to the emergency team via the 000 call feature. This will not replace standard emergency calls rather improve it through 4 major features:

Medical Drone

The dispatching of the Medical Drone when emergency call is connected and location is discovered or roughly described.

Live Instructions

Live instructions providing the crucial steps in real time provided by paramedics to treating or helping the injured person or yourself.

Live Map

Live map that provides constant updates as to the location of the Drone and Ambulance whereabouts along with estimated time of arrival.

Medical Advice

Medical advice as a secondary section of the application, providing reasons into potential issues or sicknesses, along with potential solutions or remedies to help.

PROCESS

User Research & Context Discovery

Brainstorming: Improving technology for dangerous + hard to reach situations

Our team conducted background research into the paramedic industry through the use of secondary research, semi-structured interviews, observations and semi-structured questionnaires on medical students, nurses, emergency call centre workers and paramedics.

Idea Creation

We used brainstorming and mind-mapping (who, what, when, where, why, how?) to create initial ideas and explore the problem space. Sketching was used to begin refining ideas until sketched out the solution we choose, the Dr. Drone.

One of the main sketches we based Dr Drone off

Capturing the Concept

Storyboard showing a scenario for Dr Drone

Storyboarding and creation of low fidelity sketches unable us to create the first prototypes and user test. Our testing methods of co-discovery, post test semi-structured interviews and think-aloud enabled us to generate more insights, refine issues with the problem and later pivot design to a more user friendly design.

Creation & Iteration of High Fidelities

We originally designed our high-fidelity for an android phone with the layout, but soon pivoted to an iOS look based of user feedback. The feedback enabled us to refine the key issues we faced, like the layout and hierarchy of content and the intuitive controls of the website, like swiping to go back and layout of buttons. iOS enabled us to bring our design more to life, and better address our users wants.

The Flow Diagram of High Fidelity Version 1

Reflection

I learnt about the hardships of user testing going wrong (we had issues finding the right users at points because of their busy schedules to test with), about how to be a productive team member in more intensive deadlines and overall have a greater understanding of UX Design Methods and how to create human focused interactions.
